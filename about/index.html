About Stephen

I once fried my parents desktop PC after cleaning the processor heatsink and forgetting to reconnect the fan. Around three weeks later, I shocked myself and tripped the switches in the house trying to rewire an old PC power supply. I was about eleven at the time (give or take a year), which meant I didn’t win the competition for youngest fool in Ireland but I did learn two very important lessons that I’ll never forget: if it runs hot, you really do need to keep it cool, and there’s a rather good reason PC power supplies have large “do not open” stickers on the casing.

This natural affinity towards technology led me to a degree in Computer Engineering at the University of Limerick, where I discovered software development. I undertook a great deal of research into Wireless Sensor Networks (WSNs) - a precursor or dependency of the Internet of Things (IoT) concept - as part of this degree and have worked in the virtualization, software defined networking and cloud computing areas of industry since graduating in 2013. This means I’ve been fortunate enough to work in some of the most cutting areas of the industry and you can be sure I plan to continue on this trajectory.

My code is a great source of pride and I’ve also been known to dabble in design, be that software architecture, circuit prototyping or UX. I’m happy virtually anywhere in the stack, from low level microcontroller development to backend web service development. I enjoy the “why” of my work and will happily talk for hours about the finer points of the semantic web or what “Software Craftsmanship” entails. I love to both teach and be taught and I see myself popping up in academia at some point in the future, though I’m having too much fun in industry to consider it right now.

I’m pretty happy where I am right now, but I’m always on the lookout for a way to maintain the aforementioned trajectory. If you’ve ideas here, feel free to reach out for a chat (and if you don’t, we can always talk foosball!)
